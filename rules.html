NBA Playoffs Fantasy — Rules

← Scoreboard

Objective

Draft eight NBA players — one for each playoff seed (1–8) — and accumulate the most points across the full postseason. Most total points at the end of the NBA Finals wins.

To score high, you'll need a balance of players who put up strong PPG and whose teams go deep in the bracket: a 30-PPG star who exits in round 1 can lose to a 15-PPG role player whose team reaches the Finals.

Entry

Buy-in is $50, due before tipoff of the first playoff game (TOR @ CLE, April 18th at 1:00 PM ET). Pay via Venmo to @ian-golembeski. Submit your roster through the "Enter Picks" button on the scoreboard — you can submit early and resubmit any time before lock; only your latest entry under a given name will count. The form autofills your most recent picks in-browser, so tweaks are easy. Use the "Clear" button to start from scratch.

Roster Selection

  • Each entrant picks 8 players, one per seed 1–8. A seed-slot pick may come from either the Eastern or Western Conference playoff team at that seed.
  • Different entrants may pick the same player.
  • Only players on the 16 teams that qualify for the playoffs post-play-in are eligible. Play-in rounds don't count.
  • Submit a roster via the "Enter Picks" button on the scoreboard.

Scoring

Each round, a player contributes average of their top 4 games × seed multiplier (1 + seed/10). Your total is the sum across 4 rounds × 8 picks.

Scoreboard Columns

  • Total: your cumulative fantasy points.
  • +/-: how much each pick is out-performing its cost. Per pick, (actual average PPG) − cost; summed across your 8 picks. Green = exceeding cost, red = underperforming.
  • Alive: picks still in the playoffs, out of 8.
  • PPG Rem: expected remaining upside. Σ cost × multiplier across alive picks — roughly how many points you'd earn next round if everyone ran at their season PPG.
  • Seed cells: each cell shows the seed · multiplier, player headshot, name, and current fantasy total. Hover for per-game stats.

Tiebreaker

When you submit your roster, you'll also guess the highest single-game point total any one of your 8 players will score across the entire playoffs. If two or more entrants are tied on total fantasy points at the end, whoever's guess is closest (by absolute distance) wins. A second tie falls back to earliest submission.
